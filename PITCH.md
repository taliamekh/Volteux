## 3. Q&A prep — prep notes for likely judge questions

### Q: How big is the market?

- Arduino sold over 30 million boards. SparkFun and Adafruit each ship hundreds of thousands of starter kits a year.
- Adjacent: 1.5M+ K-12 STEM classrooms in the US alone, FIRST Robotics, university intro-to-EE courses, makerspaces.
- The honest answer: we're not trying to displace Arduino IDE. We're trying to be the on-ramp for the people who buy a kit and never open it. That's the majority of buyers — Adafruit's own data shows most kits never get past the LED-blink tutorial.
- Long-term, when we add drones and robotic arms, the market is the entire hobbyist maker space, not just starter-kit beginners.

### Q: What's the moat?

- **Short answer: the 3D click-to-learn view, plus the architecture under it.**
- The component registry is a single source of truth — every part has clickable hotspots, pin-level metadata, education blurbs, and 3D model coordinates. That's hand-authored craft, not something an LLM rebuilds in a weekend.
- We have an automated eval harness that runs every generated sketch through Wokwi simulation and scores behavior correctness, not just whether it compiles. Plus a meta-harness loop that improves the prompts over time. Competitors generate code; we measure whether it actually does what the user asked.
- And honest framing: when the AI hits a project it can't reliably build, we say so — we call it the "Honest Gap." PleaseDontCode and Arduino's own AI assistant fudge. We don't. That's a trust moat that compounds.

### Q: What's next?

- **Near term (v1.5):** ESP32, Pi Pico, three more project archetypes. Manual editing — drag a component into the breadboard and the AI completes the wiring around it.
- **v2+:** Custom complex projects. Drones. Robotic arms. Animatronic pets.
- The big technical bet: AI-generated 3D-printable STL files for the mechanical parts your project needs — servo mounts, drone arms, gripper fingers. The user prints them on their printer and assembles.
- Paired with an exploded-view animation: rotate the 3D model one way, it comes apart in space; rotate the other way, it reassembles. Teaches assembly the way an iFixit teardown video does, but generated for your specific project.
- And eventually thousands of supported microcontrollers. These are real captured TODOs in our repo, not vaporware. The architecture was designed for this from day one — the registry scales, the schema scales, the eval harness scales.
